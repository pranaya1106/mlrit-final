Overview
What is Outcome Based Education?

Outcome Based Education (OBE) is a student-centric instructional approach that focuses on measuring student performance through outcomes. MLRIT has established a structured OBE framework in which Course Outcomes (COs), Program Outcomes (POs), and Program Specific Outcomes (PSOs) are defined, mapped, attained, and continuously improved. The framework is implemented across all B.Tech and M.Tech programs.

The OBE Cycle
Step-by-Step Process
1

Define Course Outcomes (COs)

Faculty define 5–6 measurable Course Outcomes for each subject at the start of every semester. COs describe what students are expected to know and demonstrate upon course completion.

2

CO–PO Mapping

Each CO is mapped to the relevant Programme Outcomes (POs) and Programme Specific Outcomes (PSOs) using a strength scale of 1 (Low), 2 (Medium), or 3 (High).

3

Select Assessment Tools

Appropriate direct and indirect assessment tools are selected to measure attainment of each CO. Internal exams, assignments, end-semester exams, and project evaluations serve as direct methods; surveys serve as indirect methods.

4

Set Target Attainment Levels

A target attainment percentage is set for each CO (typically 60% of students achieving ≥60% marks). At the PO level, the aggregate attainment target is set institution-wide.

5

Collect and Compute Attainment Data

After each semester, CO attainment is computed from assessment data. PO attainment is derived by multiplying CO attainment with CO–PO mapping strength and aggregating across all courses.

6

Analyse and Take Corrective Action

Attainment results are reviewed by the Programme Assessment and Quality Improvement Committee (PAQIC). Where targets are not met, corrective actions such as remedial classes, revised teaching methods, or additional assessments are implemented.

7

Continuous Improvement

Results are fed back into course design for the next academic cycle, ensuring continuous quality improvement aligned with NBA accreditation standards.

Direct & Indirect Methods

Direct Methods

  • Internal Examinations (Mid-1, Mid-2)
  • Assignments and Quizzes
  • End Semester Examination
  • Laboratory Assessments
  • Project Evaluation
  • Seminar Presentations

Indirect Methods

  • Exit Survey (final year students)
  • Alumni Survey
  • Employer Survey
  • Course-end Feedback
  • Peer Evaluation

Dissemination
How Outcomes Are Communicated

Programme Outcomes are shared through multiple channels to ensure all stakeholders are informed:

  • College website and department pages
  • HOD and faculty rooms (notice boards)
  • Department notice boards and classrooms
  • Syllabus booklets distributed to students
  • Laboratory manuals and course materials
